About

Period Pouch

Period cramps affect around 80% percent of
women around the world. 5-10% of women
report the pain being bad enough to interrupt
daily life. Women around the world power
through the pain.

As a woman and founder of Period Pouch, I
understand the struggles. Having experienced
these situations, women need better solutions
and more convenient solutions.

Innovation

Period Pouch works due to an exothermic reaction
between the reactants in the pouch. Iron powder
reacts with oxygen as the packaging is opened by the
customer. Heat is released by the pouch and can last
up to 10 hours after opening the package.

01

Exothermic Reaction

How It Works

To determine how much iron (reactant) to use per unit
of Period Pouch, we started with the heat released
from an average-sized sized warmer (412.1 kJ/mol).
Because the Period Pouch is around five times the
size, all calculations were based on the Period Pouch
producing five times the amount of heat a regular
hand warmer produces. Amounts of salt, vermiculite,
and activated charcoal are all around 1-10% of the
warmers weight, so per unit of Period Pouch, there
should be 1-10 grams of all other ingredients,

How is it made?

Materials

Inside the Period Pouch, there is
iron powder, This is the main
reactant that causes the heat to
be released.

01

Iron Powder (100g)

This reactant will help evenly spread
out the heat that is released by the iron
when it reacts with the oxygen.

02

Activated Charcoal (1g)

The reactant is called vermiculite.
This reactant absorbs the water that
the reaction produces to help ensure
that the heat will last longer.

03

Vermiculite (10g)

The last reactant is salt. Salt is the catalyst
of the reaction. It speeds up the reaction
between iron and oxygen. It ensures heat
at a quick speed.

04

Salt (2g)
